About the Author

Murtadha Aalim Onifade (Abu Sumayyah)

Founder, Al-Khairy Foundation

Murtadha Aalim Onifade, known as Abu Sumayyah, is a passionate advocate for Muslim youth development with over 15 years of experience in education, mentorship, and community building.

His journey began with teaching Islamic studies to teenagers in local mosques, where he noticed a critical gap: young Muslims excelling in either religious studies or secular education, but rarely both. This observation sparked a mission to develop holistic approaches to Muslim youth development.

Before founding Al-Khairy Foundation, Abu Sumayyah worked as an educational consultant, helping schools integrate Islamic values with modern pedagogy. He has conducted youth development workshops across Nigeria and consulted for Islamic schools seeking to improve both academic and Islamic education outcomes.

"Blueprint to Brilliance" distills his years of experience working with Muslim youth into a practical guide that parents, educators, and young Muslims themselves can use to navigate the challenges of modern education and career planning while remaining firmly grounded in Islamic principles.

"Our youth are not just the future of the Ummah—they are its present. When we equip them with both Islamic knowledge and worldly skills, guided by faith and purpose, we don't just change individual lives; we transform communities and ultimately, by Allah's permission, the entire Ummah."

- Murtadha Aalim Onifade

Chapter 1 Preview: Understanding Barakah

"Barakah is not merely a religious concept; it is the practical manifestation of divine blessing in our daily lives. When a student studies with Barakah, they understand more in less time. When a professional works with Barakah, their efforts yield results beyond their calculations. When wealth is acquired with Barakah, it satisfies more than its quantity suggests."

"The quest for Barakah begins with intention. Before opening a textbook, starting a project, or choosing a career path, the Muslim asks: Is this pleasing to Allah? Will this bring me closer to Him? Does this serve the Ummah? These questions transform mundane activities into acts of worship and channels for Barakah."

"Many young Muslims struggle with the perceived conflict between 'religious' success and 'worldly' success. This is a false dichotomy. The companions of the Prophet (peace be upon him) were businessmen, scholars, soldiers, and leaders—all while being among the most devoted worshippers. Their secret was not in separating these domains but in integrating them through Barakah."

Chapter Features:

  • Barakah Self-Assessment: Evaluate current activities for Barakah potential
  • Practical Exercise: Transform one daily activity into a Barakah-seeking endeavor
  • Companion Spotlight: How Umar ibn Al-Khattab (RA) infused Barakah into governance
  • Parent Guide: Helping children recognize and seek Barakah
